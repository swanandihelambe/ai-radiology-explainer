Report 4: Right Lower Lobe Pneumonia
Exam: Chest, 2 Views (PA and Lateral)
History: Cough, fever, and productive sputum for 3 days. Elevated WBC.

Findings:
There is a focal, patchy airspace consolidation within the right lower lobe, best visualized on the lateral view where it obscures the posterior hemidiaphragm (spine sign). Air bronchograms are subtly present within the consolidation. The upper right lobe and the entire left lung are clear. The cardiac silhouette is within normal limits. There are no pleural effusions or pneumothoraxes. The hila are unremarkable.

Impression:
Focal consolidation in the right lower lobe, consistent with acute bacterial pneumonia in this clinical setting.
