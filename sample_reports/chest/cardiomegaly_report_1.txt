Report 2: Cardiomegaly with Mild Pulmonary Venous Congestion
Exam: Chest, single upright view (AP)
History: History of CHF. Shortness of breath, rule out pulmonary edema.

Findings:
There is moderate enlargement of the cardiac silhouette. The pulmonary vasculature shows cephalization, consistent with mild pulmonary venous congestion. There are no focal alveolar consolidations. No large pleural effusions are identified, although mild blunting of the costophrenic angles is present bilaterally, suggestive of trace fluid. No pneumothorax. A single-chamber permanent pacemaker is noted in the left prepectoral region with a single lead positioned in the right ventricle, appearing intact.

Impression:

Moderate cardiomegaly with mild pulmonary venous congestion.

Changes are consistent with early or mild congestive heart failure.
