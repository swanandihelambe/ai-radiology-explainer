Report 3: Large Right Pleural Effusion
Exam: Chest, 2 Views (PA and Lateral)
History: New onset shortness of breath, diminished breath sounds on the right.

Findings:
There is a large, dense opacification of the right hemithorax, resulting in obscuration of the right hemidiaphragm and costophrenic angle. This density demonstrates a characteristic meniscus sign superiorly. There is subtle contralateral mediastinal shift to the left. The left lung field remains clear without consolidation or effusion. The heart is not enlarged. The visualized osseous structures are intact.

Impression:
Large right pleural effusion. The underlying right lung parenchyma is obscured. Correlation with thoracentesis or follow-up scanning after drainage may be indicated if clinically warranted.
