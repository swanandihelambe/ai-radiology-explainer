Report 5: Left-Sided Pneumothorax
Exam: Chest, Single Upright View (PA)
History: Post-thoracentesis, sudden onset chest pain and acute dyspnea.

Findings:
A distinct, thin, visceral pleural line is identified parallel to the chest wall in the superior and lateral aspects of the left hemithorax. There is a complete absence of peripheral bronchovascular lung markings peripheral to this line. The degree of collapse is estimated at approximately 20-25%. There is no dynamic mediastinal shift toward the right hemithorax to suggest tension. The right lung is clear. The cardiac silhouette is normal.

Impression:
Acute left-sided pneumothorax, approximately 20-25% volume collapse. No tension components.
